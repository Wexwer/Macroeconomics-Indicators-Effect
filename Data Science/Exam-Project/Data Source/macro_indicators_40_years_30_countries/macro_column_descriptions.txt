COLUMN DESCRIPTIONS — Macro Indicators Dataset
===============================================
40 Years · 30 Countries · 8 Regions · Annual + Monthly Series
Author: Sergey Nefedov (sergionefedov)

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
FILE 1: macro_indicators.csv  (1,200 rows — annual, 30 countries × 40 years)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

country           Country name (30 countries, see list below)
region            Geographic region:
                    NorthAmerica / Europe / EasternEurope /
                    Asia / AsiaPacific / LatAm / MiddleEast / Africa
dev_level         Development classification:
                    developed  — OECD high-income economies
                    emerging   — Emerging market economies
year              Calendar year (1985–2024)

── GDP ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
gdp_growth        Real GDP growth rate (% YoY)
                  Calibrated ranges:
                    Developed: avg +1.8%, crisis years: -4% to -6%
                    Emerging:  avg +4.5%, range: -5% to +12%
gdp_level         GDP index (1985 = 100 for each country)
                  Compound of annual growth; allows cross-country
                  growth trajectory comparison over 40 years.

── Inflation ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
inflation         Consumer Price Index, year-over-year change (%)
                  Calibrated to documented disinflation trends:
                    Developed: high (~6%) in 1980s → low (~1-2%) 1995-2021
                              → spike (~7-9%) in 2021-2022 (post-COVID)
                    Emerging:  wide range, 3-70%+ for high-inflation episodes
                  Argentina: calibrated to documented hyperinflation episodes

── Monetary Policy ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
policy_rate       Central bank benchmark interest rate (%)
                  Follows documented rate cycle:
                    USA Fed Funds Rate: 8% (1985) → 5.25% (2023)
                    ECB: 0% (2014-2022) → 4.5% (2023)
                    Emerging: higher baseline, wider variance

── Labour Market ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
unemployment      Unemployment rate (% of labour force)
                  Calibrated country-specific baselines:
                    USA: structural ~4-5%; peak 10% (2009), 14% (2020 COVID)
                    Europe: higher structural (Spain ~15-25% in crisis)
                    Emerging: typically 4-8%, informality not captured

── External Balance ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
current_account   Current account balance (% of GDP)
                  Positive = surplus (Germany, China, Netherlands)
                  Negative = deficit (USA, UK, India)
                  Range: -10% to +8%

── Fiscal ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
debt_to_gdp       Government gross debt as % of GDP
                  Notable calibrations:
                    Japan: highest (~200-250%)
                    USA: rising from ~50% (1985) to ~120% (2024)
                    Emerging: wide range 30-100%

── Currency ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
fx_change         Exchange rate change vs USD (%, annual)
                  Positive = local currency appreciated vs USD
                  Negative = depreciation
                  Emerging currencies show higher volatility;
                  Argentina/Turkey: large episodic depreciations

── Sentiment & Stress ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
biz_confidence    Business confidence index (composite, 0-100)
                  Based on PMI-style survey methodology
                  50 = neutral; >50 = expansion; <50 = contraction

fin_stress_idx    Financial stress index (0–3)
                  0.0 = normal conditions
                  1.0 = elevated stress (e.g. credit spread widening)
                  2.0 = high stress (e.g. 2008, 2020 COVID)
                  3.0 = systemic crisis
                  Calibrated to: OECD Composite Indicator of Systemic Stress (CISS)
                  and St. Louis Fed Financial Stress Index

── Events ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
shock_event       Label for major shock years (null if normal year):
                    "Asian Crisis 1997"
                    "Dot-com bust 2001"
                    "Global Financial Crisis 2008-09"
                    "European Debt Crisis 2010-12"
                    "COVID-19 2020"
                    "Post-COVID inflation 2021-22"
                    "Ukraine War / Energy shock 2022"

FILE 2: macro_monthly.csv  (480 rows — monthly, USA aggregate, 1985–2024)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

date              Date (YYYY-MM-DD), first day of month
year              Calendar year
month             Month number (1–12)

── Activity ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
gdp_growth_mom    GDP growth proxy, month-over-month (%)
                  Monthly interpolation of annual GDP data

── Inflation ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
cpi_mom           CPI month-over-month change (%)
cpi_yoy           CPI year-over-year change (%)
                  Both calibrated to US CPI historical series:
                  Peak: ~9% YoY in Jun-Jul 2022 (post-COVID)
                  Deflation: 2009, 2015 (mild)

── Monetary ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
policy_rate       Federal Funds Rate (%) at month-end
                  Key historical levels:
                    8.0% (1985) → 3.0% (1992) → 6.5% (2000) →
                    1.0% (2003) → 5.25% (2006) → 0.25% (2009-2015) →
                    2.5% (2018) → 0.25% (2020) → 5.5% (2023)

unemployment      US unemployment rate (%)
                  Monthly series calibrated to Bureau of Labor Statistics

── Leading Indicators ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
pmi               Purchasing Managers' Index (composite, manufacturing + services)
                  50 = neutral; >50 = expansion; <50 = contraction
                  Calibrated to ISM PMI historical series

yield_spread_10_2 US Treasury yield spread: 10-year minus 2-year (percentage points)
                  Positive = normal (upward sloping curve)
                  Negative = inverted curve = RECESSION PREDICTOR
                  Notable inversions: 1989, 2000, 2006-07, 2019, 2022-23

── Financial Conditions ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
vix               CBOE Volatility Index (implied 30-day S&P 500 volatility)
                  Historical range: 9 (ultra-low 2017) to 80+ (Mar 2020 COVID)
                  Key spikes: 1987 (Black Monday), 1998 (LTCM),
                              2008-09 (GFC), 2020 (COVID)

credit_spread_bps US Investment Grade credit spread vs Treasuries (basis points)
                  Low spreads (~80-100 bps) = risk-on
                  High spreads (~500-600 bps) = stress (2008-09, 2020)
                  1 basis point = 0.01%

oil_change_yoy    Brent crude oil price, year-over-year change (%)
                  Major moves:
                    1986: -50% (Saudi price war)
                    1990: +70% (Gulf War)
                    2008: +90% then -60%
                    2014-15: -50% (shale glut)
                    2020: -40% (COVID demand collapse)
                    2022: +60% (Ukraine war)

── Events ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
shock_event       Major shock label (null if normal month)
                  Same taxonomy as macro_indicators.csv

CALIBRATION SOURCES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

IMF World Economic Outlook Database (April 2024 edition)
  → GDP growth, inflation, current account, debt/GDP for all 30 countries
  → Available: https://www.imf.org/en/Publications/WEO

World Bank Open Data
  → Unemployment rates, GDP levels, development classification
  → Available: https://data.worldbank.org

FRED (Federal Reserve Bank of St. Louis)
  → US monthly series: Fed Funds Rate, CPI, unemployment, yield spread
  → VIX (from CBOE via FRED), credit spreads (BofA ICE indices)
  → Available: https://fred.stlouisfed.org

BIS Statistics (Bank for International Settlements)
  → Policy rates cross-country, credit spreads, financial stress indicators
  → Available: https://www.bis.org/statistics

OECD Statistics
  → Business confidence indices, PMI-style composite indicators
  → Available: https://stats.oecd.org

EIA (US Energy Information Administration)
  → Brent crude oil historical prices
  → Available: https://www.eia.gov/petroleum/data.php

SUGGESTED TASKS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Recession prediction (macro_monthly.csv):
  Target: binary recession indicator (derive from gdp_growth_mom < 0 for 2+ months)
  Features: yield_spread_10_2 (inverted curve), pmi, credit_spread_bps, vix
  Key insight: yield curve inversion precedes recession by 12-18 months on average

Cross-country growth regression (macro_indicators.csv):
  Target: gdp_growth
  Features: inflation, policy_rate, debt_to_gdp, current_account, fin_stress_idx
  Challenge: control for country fixed effects and global shocks simultaneously

Inflation regime analysis:
  Cluster countries into inflation regimes (low/moderate/high/hyperinflation)
  Track regime transitions over 40 years
  Which variables best predict regime change?

Currency crisis detection:
  Target: large fx_change events (|fx_change| > 15%)
  Features: current_account, debt_to_gdp, policy_rate, fin_stress_idx
  Event study: do macro imbalances predict currency crises 1-2 years ahead?

Developed vs Emerging comparison:
  Do the same monetary transmission mechanisms apply in both groups?
  Does higher policy_rate reduce inflation more effectively in developed markets?

Time series forecasting (macro_monthly.csv):
  Forecast cpi_yoy 6 months ahead using ARIMA / LSTM
  Use yield_spread_10_2 and pmi as leading indicators
  Evaluate performance across different inflation regimes

NOTE ON SYNTHETIC DATA
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

All data is synthetic. Marginal distributions and key pairwise
correlations (e.g. policy_rate → inflation, fin_stress_idx → gdp_growth)
are calibrated to the sources above. Individual country-year values
should not be cited as factual; use for modelling, education and
methodology development only.

Reproducible via generate_macro.py with seed numpy.random.default_rng(42).
